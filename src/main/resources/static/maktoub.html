Maktoub

مكتوب - It is written

Maktoub (مكتوب) is an Arabic word meaning "it is written" or "destiny." In the context of our association, Maktoub represents our commitment to documenting, preserving, and sharing the stories, scholarship, and cultural heritage of the Islamic world and the Middle East.

This section serves as a platform for student writing, research, reflections, and creative works related to Islamic and Middle East studies. We believe that every voice matters and that through writing, we can contribute to a deeper understanding of our field and our communities.

Contribute to Maktoub

We Welcome Submissions

Maktoub is open to submissions from all students interested in Islamic and Middle East studies. We accept various forms of writing including:

  • Academic essays and research papers
  • Book reviews
  • Personal reflections and narratives
  • Opinion pieces and commentary
  • Creative writing (poetry, short stories)
  • Research summaries and abstracts

Submission Guidelines

  • Submissions should be related to Islamic and/or Middle East studies
  • Academic essays should be properly cited and referenced
  • Length: 500-3000 words (flexible for creative works)
  • Please include a brief author bio (2-3 sentences)
  • Submissions can be in English, French, or Arabic

Ready to Submit?

Send us your writing and join the conversation. We're excited to read your work and share it with our community.
